## 🔭 The Big Question This Week

### OpenAI and Anthropic launched subsidy wars on the same day — will AI coding tools "burn" their way to a winner like food delivery apps?

May 14th was quite a day.

OpenAI announced: enterprises migrating from Claude Code to Codex get 2 months free (worth ~$400). Hours later, Anthropic fired back: Claude Code monthly quotas increased by 50% for all paid users.

Within 3 hours, 2,000 developers contacted OpenAI about migrating. Sam Altman personally championed Codex as "the strongest AI coding product on the market." Anthropic simultaneously launched Opus 4.7 Fast mode.

This looks eerily like the 2015 ride-hailing subsidy wars — where Didi and Kuaidi burned cash until they merged.

**But AI coding tools compete on different fundamentals than ride-sharing.**

Ride-sharing platforms derive their moat from network effects — more drivers attract more riders attract more drivers. Coding tools, however, lock users through **workflow gravity**: once developers build their daily routines on a specific tool (custom prompts, trained memories, configured skills), switching costs grow exponentially.

This is why OpenAI isn't poaching "users" — they're exploiting a "migration window." Their bet: two months is enough time for developers to rebuild their workflows in Codex, after which they won't leave. Anthropic's 50% quota boost is pure defensive logic: increase the incumbent advantage so there's no incentive to deal with migration friction.

From a scientific thinking perspective: **this is a textbook "switching cost game."** Economist Paul Klempner proved decades ago that in high-switching-cost markets, incumbents gradually raise prices on existing users while challengers increase subsidies for new ones. The equilibrium isn't winner-take-all — it's market bifurcation into two highly sticky user camps.

For creative/design teams like ours, this means one thing:

**Right now is the golden window to harvest free value.** These subsidies will likely vanish in two months, but the permanent quota increases will remain. Strategy: apply for both Codex's free trial AND Claude Code's boost simultaneously. A/B test which fits your workflow over two months — then commit before subsidies expire.

The deeper insight: when AI coding tools become daily infrastructure like IDEs, **you're not just choosing a tool — you're choosing an AI thinking style.** Codex is faster and more aggressive; Claude Code is deeper and more stable. This choice will retroactively shape your coding habits and project architecture.

It's like choosing Vim vs. Emacs — once muscle memory forms, you never want to switch.
