### 🔴 [GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra Proves 50-Year-Old Math Conjecture in Under 1 Hour with 64 Subagents](https://www.ithome.com/0/975/123.htm)
**Source**: OpenAI CDN [🏢 Official] [🟩 T1] | **Date**: July 10 | ✅ Verified: OpenAI official PDF + Ethan Knight on X + QbitAI + mathematician Thomas Bloom's public review

OpenAI announced on July 10 that GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra independently produced a complete proof of the Cycle Double Cover Conjecture in under one hour. The conjecture, independently posed by Seymour and Szekeres in 1979, had remained unsolved for 47 years. GPT-5.6 deployed 64 parallel subagents exploring different proof strategies (nowhere-zero flows, cycle-continuous mappings, ear decompositions, etc.), with dedicated adversarial agents tasked with finding gaps. The resulting proof spans just 3 pages using only classical tools predating 1985. University of Manchester mathematician Thomas Bloom called it "a very beautiful proof" but noted it cites no existing literature and has not been formally verified in Lean 4. Estimated compute cost: $275-$485. If peer-reviewed, this would mark the first time AI independently solved a major open problem listed on Wikipedia's "Unsolved Problems in Mathematics."

> 📰 **Storyline**: 5/28 OpenAI cracks Erdős Unit Distance Conjecture → 6/26 GPT-5.6 preview with Ultra mode → 7/9 GPT-5.6 GA → **7/10 64-agent parallel proof of CDC Conjecture**

### 🟡 [Google Gemma 4 Technical Report: Full Apache 2.0 Open Source, Encoder-Free Multimodal Architecture](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02770)
**Source**: arXiv [🏢 Official] [🟩 T1] | **Date**: July 11 | ✅ Verified: arXiv paper + Google DeepMind official + 300+ authors

Google DeepMind released the Gemma 4 technical report (arXiv:2607.02770) on July 11, with the full 2.3B-31B parameter family under Apache 2.0 license. The key innovation is the 12B encoder-free unified multimodal architecture — a 35M-parameter projection layer maps raw pixels and audio waveforms directly into the LLM embedding space, eliminating separate vision/audio encoders. The 31B variant scores 85.2% on multitask multilingual benchmarks, approaching closed-source models 20x its size. Supports 140+ languages, runs native audio-image-text interaction locally on a 16GB MacBook. The Gemma family has surpassed 400 million downloads with 100K+ community fine-tuned variants.
