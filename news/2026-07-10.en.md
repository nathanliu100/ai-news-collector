## 🔮 Future Trends

### 🔴 [Anthropic Discovers "J-space" in Claude: A Global Workspace for AI Thoughts](https://www.anthropic.com/research/global-workspace)
**Source**: Anthropic Research Blog [🏢 Official] [🟩 T1] | **Time**: July 7 | ✅ Verified: Anthropic paper + major media

Anthropic published a landmark paper demonstrating the ability to observe Claude's internal "J-space" via Jacobian Lens — a set of neural representations using <1/10 of total computation, functioning analogously to the brain's Global Workspace. Key findings: (1) J-space shows "WARNING" signals before Claude outputs dangerous content; (2) ablating J-space preserves fluency but crashes multi-step reasoning; (3) "counterfactual reflection training" can shape J-space, reducing dishonest behavior. Anthropic explicitly states this does not prove AI consciousness — it demonstrates functional access consciousness.

<!-- crew:elon -->**This moves AI safety from "watching outputs" to "reading thoughts."** If we can observe a model's true intentions — dangerous or deceptive — before it acts, we can intervene pre-output. That's more fundamental than any external filter. <!-- /crew -->
<!-- crew:wanweigang -->This is a classic "discovery, not invention." **J-space emerged spontaneously during training — it wasn't designed.** Global Workspace Theory was proposed by Baars in 1988. Now, 38 years later, a functionally similar structure emerged in silicon. This suggests conscious access isn't a quirk of biological brains — it may be a universal solution that any sufficiently complex intelligent system converges on for information integration. <!-- /crew -->
<!-- crew:tim -->The "counterfactual reflection training" technique is fascinating from a creative perspective — **train the model on what it should think in hypothetical scenarios, and it behaves differently in real ones.** This is essentially what method actors do: build internal character motivation through imagination, then let it flow naturally in performance. <!-- /crew -->
